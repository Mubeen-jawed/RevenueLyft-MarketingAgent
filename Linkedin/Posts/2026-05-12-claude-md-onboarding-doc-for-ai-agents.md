## Caption

We built an AI agent for our own marketing last week. The thing that made it actually work wasn't the model. It was a plain text file.

If you've tried building anything with Claude — an agent, an automation, a coding helper — you've probably hit this: it does great work, then five minutes later it forgets how you like things done, guesses where your files are, or uses a tone you'd never use.

The fix is a file called CLAUDE.md. Think of it as the onboarding doc you'd hand a new hire. Claude reads it before it starts working, so instead of re-explaining your business every single time, you write it down once.

What goes in it:

• What your company does and who you serve
• Where everything lives — folders, tools, accounts
• Who does what — if you run multiple agents, their jobs and the order they run in
• Your rules and your voice — what to always do, what to never do

Before: a sharp intern on their first day, every day.
After: someone who actually knows your business.

We do this for clients when we build automations and internal tools, and honestly, writing that context doc is half the job. Get it right and the agent stops guessing.

If you're building something with Claude and it keeps "forgetting," that's the missing piece.
