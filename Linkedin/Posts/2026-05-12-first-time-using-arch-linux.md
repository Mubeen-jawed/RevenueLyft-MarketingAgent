## Caption

I switched my main computer to Arch Linux this week. I haven't felt this much like a beginner in years.

For anyone who hasn't gone down this road: Arch is a build-it-yourself version of Linux. Almost nothing comes preinstalled. You assemble the system piece by piece, which sounds painful, and at first it is.

Day one I broke the audio. Day two I broke the wifi. Day three things started... working. And somewhere in there I realized I understood my own machine better than I had on any setup I'd used in years.

That's the part I didn't expect. When you build the thing yourself, you actually know how it works. So when it breaks, you can fix it.

It's the same reason I like building software the slow way sometimes. You trade a little speed up front for actually understanding what you've got, and that pays off every time something goes wrong later.

I'm not telling anyone to go install Arch. Most people shouldn't. But doing something that makes you a beginner again is good for you, and I'd forgotten that.

What's the last tool or skill that put you back at square one?
